Scanner Guide

How It Works

What the dashboard checks before a spread appears.

Dashboard

Purpose

Find candidates, then verify with your broker.

This app scans current option chains for put credit spreads that match your rules. It is designed to surface trade ideas quickly on your phone, not place trades or guarantee fills.

Quick Translation

Options are quoted per share, but one standard option contract controls 100 shares. A displayed credit of $1.65 usually means about $165 collected for one spread before commissions and fill differences.

Current Rules

Expiration45-60 DTE
Spread width$5 wide
Short put delta-0.15 to -0.30
Minimum credit$0.60

Trade Direction

The current scanner looks for put credit spreads. Selling a put spread is generally a bullish or neutral-to-bullish trade: you want the underlying to stay above the short put strike or move higher. A future call credit spread mode would be bearish or neutral-to-bearish, because you would want the underlying to stay below the short call strike.

What Gets Scanned

The first version scans a focused universe: SPY, QQQ, IWM, AAPL, AMZN, MSFT, NVDA, and TSLA. That list is configurable. More tickers can be added later, but a smaller universe keeps the first mobile dashboard faster and easier to trust.

How Ranking Works

Candidates are ranked by credit divided by max risk. For a $5-wide spread with $1.00 credit, max risk is $4.00, so the score is 25.0%. Higher scores mean more credit per dollar at risk, but they can also reflect higher underlying risk.

Example Sell 520 put / buy 515 put for $0.80 credit

Max risk is $4.20, so credit/risk is 19.0% before commissions and slippage.

What The Numbers Mean

Credit

The estimated premium received for opening the spread. $1.65 credit means about $165 per 1 contract spread.

Max risk

The spread width minus the credit. For a $5-wide spread with $1.65 credit, max risk is $3.35, or about $335.

Credit/risk

The premium divided by max risk. $1.65 / $3.35 = 49.3%. Higher is more premium per dollar at risk.

Try limit

The scanner's estimated mid-price credit. This is the first limit credit to try in your broker.

Do not chase below

A lower bound for the credit. If the broker cannot fill near this credit, the trade may no longer match the idea.

Breakeven

The short put strike minus the credit. The underlying needs to finish above this level at expiration to avoid a loss at settlement.

Nearby Spread Ladder

Tapping a candidate shows nearby $5-wide put spreads with the same expiration. This helps compare whether the selected strike is meaningfully better than the strikes directly above or below it.

Why Some Tickers Are Skipped

Single stocks are skipped when Yahoo reports earnings within the next 7 days or within the past 3 days. ETFs do not have company earnings, so they are treated differently.

Important Limits

  • Option Greeks are estimated with Black-Scholes when Yahoo does not provide them.
  • Fills use mid-prices, which may not be available in a live broker order ticket.
  • Yahoo/yfinance data can be delayed, missing, or temporarily unavailable.
  • The scanner is for idea generation; final entry should be checked manually.
