Scanner Guide

How It Works

What the dashboard checks before a spread appears.

Dashboard

Purpose

Find candidates, then verify with your broker.

This app scans current option chains for credit spreads that match your rules. It is designed to surface trade ideas quickly on your phone, not place trades or guarantee fills.

Update Schedule

The app refreshes on market weekdays at 9:15 AM, 12:00 PM, and 2:30 PM Central Time. The first scan is intentionally after the opening rush because option quotes can be wider and less stable during the first 30-45 minutes after the 8:30 AM CT market open. The midday scan gives a cleaner second look, and the 2:30 PM scan captures a near-close view before the market closes at 3:00 PM CT.

Quick Translation

Options are quoted per share, but one standard option contract controls 100 shares. A displayed credit of $1.65 usually means about $165 collected for one spread before commissions and fill differences.

Current Rules

Expiration45-60 DTE
Spread width$5 wide
Short Put Delta-0.15 to -0.30
Short Call Delta0.15 to 0.30
Minimum credit$0.60

A spread only appears if it passes the expiration, width, delta, credit, earnings, quote quality, and liquidity checks.

Trade Direction

The scanner looks for both put credit spreads and call credit spreads using the same structure and mirrored delta ranges. Selling a put spread is generally bullish or neutral-to-bullish: you want the underlying to stay above the short put strike or move higher. Selling a call spread is generally bearish or neutral-to-bearish: you want the underlying to stay below the short call strike or move lower.

What Gets Scanned

The first version scans a focused universe: SPY, QQQ, IWM, AAPL, AMZN, MSFT, NVDA, and TSLA. That list is configurable. More tickers can be added later, but a smaller universe keeps the first mobile dashboard faster and easier to trust.

Selection Process

  1. Pull the latest option chains for the configured tickers.
  2. Keep expirations between 45 and 60 days out.
  3. Build $5-wide put credit spreads and call credit spreads.
  4. Estimate the short-leg delta when Yahoo does not provide Greeks.
  5. Reject spreads outside the target delta range or below the minimum credit.
  6. Reject spreads with weak open interest, poor volume, or wide bid/ask quotes.
  7. Skip single stocks when earnings are too close.
  8. Rank the remaining candidates by credit divided by max risk.

How Ranking Works

Candidates are ranked by credit divided by max risk. For a $5-wide spread with $1.00 credit, max risk is $4.00, so the score is 25.0%. Higher scores mean more credit per dollar at risk, but they can also reflect higher underlying risk.

Example Sell 520 put / buy 515 put for $0.80 credit

Max risk is $4.20, so credit/risk is 19.0% before commissions and slippage.

Higher credit/risk is useful, but it is not automatically better. A very high score can also mean the market is pricing in more risk, so liquidity, trend, earnings, and the nearby spread ladder still matter.

What the Numbers Mean

Credit

The estimated premium received for opening the spread. $1.65 credit means about $165 per 1 contract spread.

Max Risk

The spread width minus the credit. For a $5-wide spread with $1.65 credit, max risk is $3.35, or about $335.

Credit/risk

The premium divided by max risk. $1.65 / $3.35 = 49.3%. Higher is more premium per dollar at risk.

Try Limit

The scanner's estimated mid-price credit. This is the first limit credit to try in your broker.

Do Not Chase Below

A lower bound for the credit. If the broker cannot fill near this credit, the trade may no longer match the idea.

Breakeven

For puts, breakeven is the short put strike minus the credit. For calls, breakeven is the short call strike plus the credit.

Nearby Spread Ladder

Tapping a candidate shows nearby $5-wide spreads with the same expiration and strategy type. This helps compare whether the selected strike is meaningfully better than the strikes directly above or below it.

Why Some Tickers Are Skipped

Single stocks are skipped when Yahoo reports earnings within the next 7 days or within the past 3 days. ETFs do not have company earnings, so they are treated differently.

Important Limits

  • Option Greeks are estimated with Black-Scholes when Yahoo does not provide them.
  • Fills use mid-prices, which may not be available in a live broker order ticket.
  • Yahoo/yfinance data can be delayed, missing, or temporarily unavailable.
  • The scanner is for idea generation; final entry should be checked manually.
